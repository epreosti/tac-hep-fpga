Part a) Baseline - No Pragmas
Vitis HLS auto-pipelined the loop to II=1 without any directives, giving 66 cycles latency, 6 DSPs, 19 FFs, 193 LUTs. The DSPs come from the multiplications in D[i] and E[i].

Part b) m_axi Interface
Adding m_axi interfaces increased latency slightly to 79 cycles due to AXI overhead and worsened the clock to 18.250 ns. FF and LUT usage jumped a lot (FF=3516, LUT=3978, BRAM=10).

Part c) Pipeline II=1
Explicit pipelining gave identical results to part a) since HLS had already auto-pipelined the loop. 

Part d) Partitioning Comparison
All strategies achieved the same latency (66 cycles). Complete partitioning uses the most LUTs (720) but achieves the fastest clock (4.801 ns) by converting arrays to registers. Cyclic (factor=4) adds FFs (62) for banked access logic but uses fewer LUTs (227). Reshape is identical to baseline since II=1 was already achieved.
