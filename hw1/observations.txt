Observations:

Vector addition and matrix addition use no DSP blocks since addition
maps purely to LUTs. Vector and matrix multiplication use DSP blocks
(3 and 6 respectively) for the multiply operations. Matrix multiplication
is the most resource-intensive (LUT=893, FF=227) due to the triple-nested
loop, compared to the simpler operations (LUT<220, FF<34). Vector add,
vector mul, and matrix add all have the same latency of 102 cycles with
II=1. Matrix multiplication is much slower at 502 cycles with II=5 due
to the loop-carried dependency in the accumulation step. Fmax is highest
for the addition-based designs (267 MHz) and lowest for matrix
multiplication (~120 MHz).
