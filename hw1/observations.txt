Vector and matrix addition do not use DSP blocks because additions are implemented with LUTs. Vector and matrix multiplication use DSPs for the multiply operations (3 DSPs and 6 DSPs, respectively). Matrix multiplication also uses the most resources because of its triple nested loop, while the other operations remain much simpler.
Vector add, vector multiply, and matrix add all achieve a latency of 102 cycles with II = 1. Matrix multiplication is much slower at 502 cycles with II = 5.
The addition-based designs also reach the highest clock frequency (~267 MHz), while matrix multiplication has the lowest at about 120 MHz.
